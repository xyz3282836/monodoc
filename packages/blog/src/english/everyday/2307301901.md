New research suggests humans lived in South America at the same time as now `extinct` giant `sloths`, bolstering evidence that people arrived in the Americas earlier than once thought. Scientists analyzed `triangular` and teardrop-shaped `pendants` made of `bony` material from the sloths. They concluded that the `carved` and `polished` shapes and `drilled` holes were the work of `deliberate` craftsmanship. Dating of the `ornaments` and `sediment` at the Brazil site where they were found point to an age of 25,000 to 27,000 years ago, the researchers reported.
